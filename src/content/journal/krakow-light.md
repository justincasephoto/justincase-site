There is a particular quality of light on winter afternoons in Kraków. It appears around three o'clock and disappears without warning by half past four. It is the colour of old gold — the same gold you see in the icon paintings at the Czartoryski Museum — and it falls at an angle that makes every cobblestone into a small drama.

I spent yesterday in Podgórze. I walked for two hours without taking a single photograph I wanted to keep. This is normal. Street photography is mostly failure. The person you tracked across a square turns around too soon; the light changes; the dog moves. You wait, you miss it, you wait again.

Then, just before I decided to go home, a woman walked under the bridge on Kącik Street. The backlight caught the left side of her face and left the right in complete shadow. Her coat — dark wool, decades old — absorbed everything except that one stripe of gold across her cheekbone.

One frame. Out of focus on the second. The third, she was gone.

This is the whole game.
